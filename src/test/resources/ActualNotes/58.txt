THE REPORT RECEIVED FROM THE AFFILIATE INDICATED THAT APPROXIMATELY TWENTY-FIVE AND ONE-HALF MONTHS (25 1/2) AFTER THE INDEX PROCEDURE, THE PATIENT COMPLAINED OF CHEST PAIN AND COLLAPSED. THE PATIENT WAS REPORTED TO BE IN CARDIOPULMONARY ARREST WHEN THE AMBULANCE ARRIVED. CARDIOPULMONARY RESUSCITATION WAS CONDUCTED AND THE PATIENT WAS RESUSCITATED. THE PATIENT WAS TRANSFERRED TO THE HOSPITAL BY AMBULANCE. CORONARY ANGIOGRAPHY WAS DONE AND REVEALED THROMBUS IN THE TWO (2) PREVIOUSLY IMPLANTED CYPHER STENTS IN AND DISTAL TO THE PROXIMAL/MID LEFT ANTERIOR DESCENDING (LAD) AND ALSO IN THE PROXIMAL CIRCUMFLEX. THE VERY LATE THROMBOSIS (VLT) WAS TREATED BY ASPIRATION USING A THROMBUSTER 8 FR., AND ALSO BY BALLOON ANGIOPLASTY USING A LONG BALLOON REPEATEDLY. THE FLOW DID NOT RECOVER AND THE PATIENT EXPIRED. NO AUTOPSY WAS DONE. THE PHYSICIAN'S COMMENT REGARDING THE POSSIBLE CAUSE OF THE THROMBOTIC EVENT WAS THAT IT WAS UNKNOWN AS THE EVENT OCCURRED MORE THAN TWO (2) YEARS AFTER IMPLANTATION OF THE CYPHER STENTS.
